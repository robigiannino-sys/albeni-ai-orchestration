### Paid Strategy Models (Relevance for Albeni)

1. **Organic-First** (Asket, Outlier): Near-zero paid, content-driven. Proves organic works but requires time + authority.
2. **DTC Acquisition** (Unbound Merino): High paid investment for direct acquisition. Closest to Albeni's Phase 1 needs.
3. **Selective Brand** (Allbirds, Wool&Prince): Low paid, focused on brand terms. Suitable for Phase 3.
4. **Aggressive Full-Funnel** (Icebreaker): 721 KW, 27.5K traffic. Out of reach for €30K budget.

**Recommended model for Albeni**: Start with DTC Acquisition (Phase 1), transition to Selective Brand (Phase 3). Never attempt Aggressive Full-Funnel.

## Competitive Gaps — Albeni's Opportunities

1. **"Invisible Luxury" is uncontested**: No competitor owns the luxury merino outer garment space with Italian heritage
2. **Cut & Sew moat**: Nobody competes on "woven merino t-shirt" (vs knitted). Semantic Defense territory.
3. **AI Visibility first-mover**: No competitor has optimized for AI search. Score range 20-55 with no intentional strategy.
4. **DE market underserved**: English-language competitors weak in Germany. MT+PE pipeline gives Albeni a structural advantage.
5. **Multi-domain SERP domination**: No competitor uses ecosystem approach — potential for multiple domain rankings on same queries.
6. **Declining competitors = keyword capture**: Icebreaker (-1.9%), Allbirds (-1.6%), Westernrise (-5.8%), Wool&Prince (-7.4%) are losing positions.

## Competitive Threats

1. **Authority gap**: Even Tier 3 has Auth 30+. Albeni needs 12+ months of link building.
2. **Icebreaker paid aggression**: 721 KW, 27.5K traffic. €30K budget cannot compete head-to-head.
3. **Allbirds backlink moat**: 172K from 16K domains. Takes years to approach.
4. **Market contraction signals**: Multiple leaders declining — could indicate market shift or saturation.
