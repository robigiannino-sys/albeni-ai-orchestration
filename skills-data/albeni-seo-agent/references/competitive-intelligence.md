## Competitive Gaps (Opportunities for Albeni)

1. **Cut & Sew niche**: No competitor owns the "woven merino t-shirt" (vs knitted) space. Albeni's Semantic Defense (17-micron Reda fabric, cut & sew construction) is an uncontested territory.

2. **Italian Heritage**: No competitor has genuine Italian textile heritage (1905). "Made in Italy" merino is a differentiated positioning none of them can claim.

3. **AI Visibility**: The segment is relatively low on AI presence (max 55 for Smartwool). Early investment in structured content could capture AI search share disproportionately.

4. **DE market**: Germany is underserved by English-language competitors. Albeni's planned DE content (via MT+PE) could capture an undercontested market.

5. **Multi-domain authority**: No competitor uses a multi-domain ecosystem strategy. This gives Albeni potential SERP domination for its core terms (multiple domains ranking for the same query family).

## Competitive Threats

1. **Authority gap**: Even Tier 2 competitors have Authority 30-43. Albeni needs 12+ months of consistent link building to compete.

2. **Icebreaker's paid aggression**: 721 paid KW with 27.5K traffic. If Icebreaker targets Albeni's niches, the €30K budget cannot compete head-to-head.

3. **Allbirds' backlink moat**: 172K backlinks from 16K domains. This kind of profile takes years to build.

4. **Declining trends**: Icebreaker and Allbirds show declining organic. This could mean the market is shifting or could mean opportunity as they lose ground.
