## Paid → Organic Transition Strategy (Aggiornamento 2026-04-05)

This replaces the generic Phase 1/2/3 budget scaling with a keyword-level transition model. The core insight: not all keywords transition from paid to organic at the same speed. Low-KD keywords transition fastest — concentrate paid budget there first.

### Priority KW for Paid → Organic Handoff

| Keyword | Volume | KD% | Landing | Pillar | Transition Time |
|---------|--------|-----|---------|--------|-----------------|
| **merino wool base layer** | 29K | 23% | PMS + WoM 2090 | L1 | 6-9 months |
| **merino wool t shirt** | 6.2K | 22% | PMS + Albeni | L1 | 6-9 months |

These two keywords have the best volume/KD ratio in the entire L1 set. With KD <25%, strong content + interlinking + modest backlink investment can reach top 5 in 6-9 months.

### How the Transition Works

1. **Phase A (M1-M6):** Paid carries 100% of traffic for this keyword. Budget: aggressive bidding to secure top ad positions.
2. **Phase B (M4-M9):** Content matures, interlinking feeds PageRank from L2 pages, organic position climbs. Paid maintains but bids reduce as organic CTR grows.
3. **Phase C (M7-M12):** Organic reaches top 5. Paid scales down to brand defense only. Budget freed up shifts to the NEXT keyword in the transition queue.

### Transition Queue (ordered by volume/KD efficiency)

1. "merino wool base layer" (29K, KD 23%) — **START NOW**
2. "merino wool t shirt" (6.2K, KD 22%) — **START NOW**
3. "capsule wardrobe" (36K, KD ~35%) — organic already strong (page 195 has 11 inbound links), paid as accelerator
4. "merino wool" (45K, KD ~45%) — long-term organic play, paid stays structural longer

When recommending budget allocation, always reference this transition queue and the current phase for each keyword. The goal is a rolling handoff: as each keyword transitions to organic, the freed budget funds the next one.
